About Michael B. Jordan

Michael B. Jordan is an American actor born on February 9, 1987, in Santa Ana, California. His parents, Donna and Michael A. Jordan, raised him and his siblings in Newark, New Jersey, where he attended Newark Arts High School. Jordan began his acting career at a young age, appearing in commercials and TV shows like "The Sopranos" and "All My Children." However, his breakout role came in 2009 when he portrayed quarterback Vince Howard in the critically acclaimed TV series "Friday Night Lights."

Following this success, Jordan landed roles in films such as "Chronicle," "Fruitvale Station," and "Creed," solidifying his status as a rising star in Hollywood. He also gained widespread recognition for his role as Erik Killmonger in the 2018 Marvel film "Black Panther."

Jordan has been praised for his acting skills and his dedication to his craft, often undergoing intense physical training for his roles. In addition to acting, he is also a producer, having worked on projects like "Raising Dion" and "David Makes Man."

Outside of his professional career, Jordan is known for his philanthropic efforts, particularly in support of the Black Lives Matter movement and social justice causes. He has also been involved in initiatives to increase diversity and representation in Hollywood.

Despite his success and fame, Jordan has remained grounded and dedicated to his craft, continually pushing himself to take on new challenges and expand his abilities as an actor and producer.
